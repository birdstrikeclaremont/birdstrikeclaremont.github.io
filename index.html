Preventing Bird Strike at the Claremont Colleges

For birds, glass windows are worse than invisible. By reflecting foliage or sky, they look like inviting places to fly into. And because the sheer number of windows is so great, their toll on birds is huge. Up to about 1 billion birds die from window strikes in the U.S. each year, according to a 2014 study.

Window collisions are responsible for over three billion bird deaths each year. This kills approximately six percent of the global population of our feathered friends.

What can we do at the 5C's and at home to mitigate bird casualties?

Firstly, help our 5C scientists by reporting any downed birds you see around campus. Click the red button to fill out a quick form. This gives us crucial information about potential problem spots on our campus!

Secondly, simple and easy to apply patterns can break up the reflections on windows that confuse birds. This can be as simple as adding a window screen or some reflective tape, or as creative as painting bird-saving designs to liven up your home. It is important that these modifications are added to the outside of the glass... sadly decals on the inside will not help our feathery friends.

Lastly, one simple thing is to turn the lights off at night. 80% of birds migrate at night and can be dragged off course and into danger by light pollution. Turn off your lights, especially during the migration season. Lights Out for birds movements operate in numerous U.S. cities, saving energy, money, and, of course, our birds.
